# Retail Inventory Performance Dashboard

## Project Overview

This project analyzes retail inventory performance, revenue contribution, and discount effectiveness using SQL Server and Power BI.

The objective was to solve common retail business problems related to inventory management, product demand, pricing strategies, and category performance. SQL was used for data analysis and business problem-solving, while Power BI was used to create an interactive dashboard for business insights and decision-making.

---

## Business Problems Solved

### 1. Inventory Risk Analysis
Identified products with low stock levels and high customer engagement to highlight potential stockout risks and support inventory replenishment decisions.

### 2. Demand vs Stock Monitoring
Analyzed the relationship between stock availability and customer demand to identify products requiring immediate attention.

### 3. Discount Effectiveness Analysis
Evaluated how different discount ranges impact customer engagement, ratings, and revenue generation.

### 4. Revenue Contribution Analysis
Analyzed category-wise and brand-wise revenue contribution to identify high-performing segments.

### 5. Executive Performance Monitoring
Created an executive summary dashboard to provide a consolidated view of revenue, product performance, customer ratings, and engagement metrics.

## Key SQL Analyses

### Demand vs Low Stock Risk
Used CTEs and Window Functions to identify the top high-demand products with limited inventory availability.

### Discount Impact Analysis
Grouped products into discount ranges and analyzed their effect on customer engagement using reviews and ratings.

### Revenue Contribution Analysis
Calculated estimated revenue contribution by brand and category to identify top-performing segments.

---

## Key Insights

- Electronics generated the highest overall revenue.
- Approximately 15.5% of products were classified as High or Critical inventory risk.
- Products with high reviews and low stock levels require replenishment priority.
- The 11–20% discount range generated the highest revenue and customer engagement.
- Moderate discounting appeared more effective than aggressive discounting.
